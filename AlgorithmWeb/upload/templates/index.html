  • Single  cell  Sequencing

    Single cell sequencing examines the sequence information from individual cells with optimized next generation sequencing (NGS) technologies, providing a higher resolution of cellular differences and a better understanding of the function of an individual cell in the context of its microenvironment.
    Reference: Eberwine J , Sul J Y , Bartfai T , et al. The promise of single-cell sequencing[J]. Nature Methods, 2014, 11(1):25-27.

  • A  decade  of  single-cell  RNA-seq

    In the past few years, many sensitive and accurate single-cell RNA-sequencing (scRNA-seq) protocols have been introduced. The scale in single-cell transcriptomics experiments is growing exponentially, from tens of cells up to hundreds of thousands of single cells per study in less than a decade. Many different incremental improvements have contributed to increases in scale, such as, for example, decreases in required reagent volumes and consumable costs facilitated by the introduction of microfluidic technologies, random capture methods and in situ barcoding.
    Reference: Svensson V , Vento-Tormo R , Teichmann S A . Exponential scaling of single-cell RNA-seq in the past decade[J]. Nature Protocols, 2018, 13(4):599-604.

  • Clustering  Methods

    Clustering analysis is a hot topic in the study of scRNA-seq data. There have been existed many approach or tools developed. Here we focus on 15 classical clustering methods, which were dived into 6 categories, i.e. Distance-based, Density-based, Graph-based, Probability model -based, Matrix-based and Deep learning-based.

  • Performance  comparison  among  different  methods  on  twelve  datasets

    Performance comparison among different methods on twelve datasets. Overall, SC3 achieves the best performance because it uses consensus clustering. CIDR also performs better than most other methods on most datasets as it takes dropout events in scRNA-seq into account. While the performance of SNN-Cliq is not stable as traditional distance metric cannot measure well the distance between cells in some scRNA-seq datasets. CellTree and pcaReduce perform stable with high accuracy on all these datasets. For GiniClust, only a few gene can be selected when p-value is set to 0.0001, thus it performs well only on a few datasets. DIMM-SC was designed for droplet-based scRNA-seq, so it performs better on the last two droplet-based datasets than the other methods.

  • Performance  comparison  among  different  methods  on  ten  datasets

    Performance of IGRW (Integrated clustering with Random Walk on Graph). IGRW performs better than the other methods in most of the ten real scRNA-seq datasets. Since IGRW synthesizes the results of the other four methods, it is greatly affected by the large difference between them. In detail, IGRW performs worse if most methods give false predictions.
